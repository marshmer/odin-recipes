Vegetarian Pasta Sauce with Mushrooms

Great to use on your favorite Italian dish!

Use fresh mushrooms in place of meat to create a hearty and healthy pasta sauce alternative. This goes great with our Pizzaghetti or Chicken Parm Sub recipes!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/2 pound fresh baby portobello mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 pound fresh button mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons dry red wine, or more to taste
  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans diced tomatoes with basil, garlic, and oregano
  • 1 (23 ounce) jar pasta sauce
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 pinch white sugar, or to taste
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium-heat. Add portobello mushrooms, button mushrooms, and onion. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms release their juices and onion is soft and translucent, 6 to 8 minutes.
  2. Pour in wine to deglaze the pot, scraping up any bits stuck to the bottom.
  3. Pour in diced tomatoes and cook, stirring, until liquid has slightly reduced, about 5 mins. Add pasta sauce, garlic, Italian seasoning, sugar, salt, and pepper; stir to combine. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce temperature to low and simmer, uncovered, until flavors have blended and desired thickness has been reached, about 15 mins. Stir sauce occasionally.
